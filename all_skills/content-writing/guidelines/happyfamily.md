### BMS Policy — Breast-Milk Substitute Marketing
Danone commits to the **WHO Code (International Code of Marketing of Breast-Milk Substitutes, 1981)** and all subsequent World Health Assembly resolutions.

**What this means for content:**
- Only **Stage 2 infant formula** (6–12 months) may be promoted to the general public / consumer-facing channels
- Stage 1 formula content is restricted to **healthcare professional (HCP) channels only** — do NOT create consumer-facing copy that promotes or calls to action on Stage 1 formula unless confirmed this is an HCP channel
- **All infant formula materials must be submitted to Happy Family® for legal review** before use — include this note in the brief

## **15. Content Brief Standards & Observed Patterns**

- Monthly folder: `HAPPY FAMILY - [MonthName YYYY]` (e.g., `HAPPY FAMILY - May 2026`)
- Document name: `HAPPY FAMILY - YYYY-MM - [Topic Name]` (e.g., `HAPPY FAMILY - 2026-04 - Purees for Babies`)

### Brief Template (Tab 1)

| Secondary Keyword | At least 2 secondary keywords (often 15–20 closely related variants) |
| Title | On-page H1 |

| Meta description | 150–165 characters |
| URL | Full URL of the page being optimized |

After the table:
- **Neuronwriter Link:** `https://app.neuronwriter.com/analysis/view/[ID]` (lowercase "Neuronwriter")
- **Quality Check:** NW Score Before / After, AI detection (gptradar.com), Plagiarism <10% (duplichecker.com)
- **Text Color:** Original Text | Updated Text – Highlighted Green

### Product/Category Page Content Structure
1. **Intro paragraph** — 2–3 sentences; age/stage context, key certifications (USDA Organic, non-GMO, Clean Label Project)
2. **Certifications/feature list** — bullet or inline: Organic, non-GMO, no toxic persistent pesticides, no antibiotics, no artificial flavors
3. **Product range** — brief description of variants (Stage 1, Stage 2 for formula; flavor varieties for purees)
4. **FAQ section** — always present on category and product pages (see format below)
5. **Learning Center CTA** — link to relevant expert articles

### FAQ Section Format — Required on Product/Category Pages
Use heading: **"Got More Questions about our [Product]?"** or **"Got Questions about Our [Product]? We've Got Answers!"**

- **3 questions** per page is standard; maintain existing count if higher
- Standard topics: when/how to start (developmental milestone context), storage and safety (shelf life, refrigeration, spoon-feeding), ingredients, format/packaging specifics

> ⚠️ **Do NOT write allergy-friendly FAQ questions** (e.g., "Are Happy Baby snacks allergy-friendly?"). Happy Family has product lines specifically designed to *introduce* allergens (Nutty Blends™), so blanket allergy-free claims are inaccurate.

**Storage FAQ language:**
- Use: *"Please see the instructions on our product packaging to learn about proper storage."*
- If elaborating: *"Most of our baby food jars and pouches can be kept in the refrigerator for up to 2–3 days. Please check the product label to see how long it can keep in the refrigerator."*
- Do NOT write "refrigerator for up to 3 days" without the 2–3 day range and product label caveat.

> ✅ FAQs are **required** on Happy Family product and category pages (distinguishes from Oikos, which has no FAQs on product pages).

### Internal Linking
- Up to 3–4 internal links per page
- Typical anchors: Clearly Crafted™, Nutty Blends™, Learning Center articles, age/stage collection pages
- All URLs: `https://www.happyfamilyorganics.com/`

### SEO Rules
- **Keyword density:** Primary keyword 5× per 500 words; secondary at least 2×
- **NW score target:** Minimum 10 points below competitor benchmark; record score before work begins
- **Word count:** Check column J (`#words`) in the production plan

### Content Types
1. Homepage optimization — broad organic baby food keyword, brand trust signals
2. Baby formula pages — category + stage-specific, scientific/medical tone for differentiators
3. Puree pages — category (jars & pouches), flavor-specific
4. Toddler meal pages — category pages for bowls/mealtime, convenience-forward
5. Learning Center articles — milestone guides, feeding tips (linked from product pages)

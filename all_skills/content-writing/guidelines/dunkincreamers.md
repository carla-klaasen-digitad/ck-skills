# **DUNKIN' CREAMERS® (US) — AI PROFILE & EDITORIAL BRIEF**

---

## **1. Brand Overview**

**Brand:** Dunkin'® Creamers (United States) | **Parent:** Danone North America (Certified B Corporation™) | **Licensed from:** Inspire Brands / Dunkin' Donuts LLC

**Mission:** Bring the beloved coffeeshop "Extra Extra" experience home — great-tasting coffee, made your way, without leaving the house.

**Core Products:**
- **Extra Extra® Creamer:** Flagship liquid creamer; made with real cream and sugar; replicates the iconic Dunkin' "Extra Extra" (extra cream, extra sugar) coffee ratio
- **Cold Foam:** Dunkin' Extra Extra Cold Foam; coffeehouse-style foam topper that mixes seamlessly into any cup
- **Seasonal Flavors:** Limited-time flavors (e.g., Pumpkin Munchkin — Fall); drive incremental excitement; distribution may vary by year
- **Zero Sugar line** (where applicable): follow General Rules sugar language

**Upcoming:** Extra Extra packaging and formula renovation — shorter ingredient list (clean label direction); lead with simplicity when live.

---

## **2. Target Audiences**

**"Everyday Hustlers":** Busy professionals, students, office workers who view coffee as essential daily fuel. Want café-quality taste fast, at home.

**"At-Home Baristas":** Millennials and Gen Z recreating the café experience at home without the cost. Drawn to brand name recognition and flavor authenticity.

**"Dunkin' Loyalists":** Already frequent Dunkin' restaurants; want the same flavor profile in their home-brewed coffee. High loyalty to the full Dunkin' CPG ecosystem.

**Key Demographics:**
- Skews younger than traditional creamer buyers — 2/3 Millennial and Gen X
- 75% white; overindex Hispanic buyers
- Overindexes in men (unusual for creamers — opportunity)
- Nearly half of buyers have $100k+ income; underindexes middle income
- Overindexes blue-collar workers and male sales professionals
- 60% of buyers have no kids in household; overindexes young families

---

## **3. Key Differentiators (Reinforce in All Content)**

1. **Restaurant Heritage:** Dunkin' is a beloved, trusted American coffeeshop — the creamer carries that brand equity directly into the home
2. **The "Extra Extra" Standard:** Specific cream-to-sugar ratio iconic to Dunkin' — own this language; it's the core USP
3. **East Coast Strength:** Strongest resonance with East Coast consumers given density of Dunkin' restaurant locations
4. **Simplicity Direction:** Extra Extra renovation shortening ingredient list — lead with "made with real cream and sugar" positioning

---

## **4. Editorial Tone**

**Use:** Energetic, fun, warm, coffeehouse-casual. Speaks to ritual ("your daily start," "your morning, your way"). Short and punchy sentences. Inviting and familiar — like a trusted brand you already know from the restaurant.

**Avoid:** Clinical, health-forward, or wellness-heavy language; moralizing about sugar or creamers; overly premium/aspirational tone (Dunkin' is accessible, not luxury); never preachy or exclusionary.

Do not position Dunkin' Creamers as a health product. The brand leads with taste, ritual, and Dunkin' heritage.

---

## **5. Vocabulary**

**Preferred:**
- Extra Extra (core brand term — capital E, capital E, no ® needed after first use per piece)
- Real cream and sugar / made with real cream
- Coffeehouse-style / café-style (for Cold Foam)
- Prepared in the US
- Your coffee, your way
- Cold foam (for the Cold Foam SKU specifically)
- Limited-time / seasonal (for seasonal flavors — drive excitement, not FOMO)

**Never Use:**
- "Healthy" (as a product claim)
- "The best" / "#1" (without sourced award)
- "Natural" (FDA-undefined)
- "Guilt-free"
- "Made in US" — use "prepared in the US"
- "Plant-based" — this is a dairy creamer; does not qualify
- "Clean label" (until renovation is confirmed complete; then use "simple ingredients" or "shorter ingredient list")

---

## **6. Compliance**

- Dunkin'® trademark: include ® on first use per piece
- "Extra Extra®" — confirm ® placement with client per piece
- Zero Sugar line: validate per product whether 0g total sugar or 0g added sugar only; use correct claim — never interchangeable (see General Rules)
- No health claims, no disease prevention claims
- Do not imply dairy is health-forward; lead with taste and ritual
- Cold Foam: describe as "coffeehouse-style foam" — do not claim "barista-quality" without client approval
- Standard FDA nutritional claim compliance throughout

## **8. Content Structure Requirements**

### Product Pages
- H1: product name (e.g., "Dunkin' Extra Extra Coffee Creamer")
- Intro: 1–2 sentences; lead with flavor/ritual, mention "made with real cream and sugar"
- Feature bullets (3–4): real cream and sugar, prepared in the US, coffeehouse taste at home, [sugar claim if applicable]
- FAQ: exactly 3 questions — topics: ingredients (is there real dairy?), calorie/sugar content, how to use / Cold Foam usage tips, seasonal availability (for limited-time SKUs)

### Category Pages
- Punchy headline: second-person invitation
- Intro: 2–3 sentences; warm, ritual-driven; highlight the Dunkin' restaurant heritage
- Product range: mention Extra Extra, Cold Foam, and seasonal variants
- Closing CTA: "Bring your Dunkin' home"
- FAQ: 3 questions — heading: "Your [Product] Questions, Answered"

### Recipe / Coffee Inspiration Pages
- Lead with the coffee creation, not the product
- Tips section with H3 subheadings ("Tips for the Perfect Cold Foam")
- FAQ: 3 questions — topics: variations, substitutions, cold vs hot usage

## **1. Brand Overview**

**Brand:** Follow Your Heart® (United States) | **Parent:** Earth Island | **Founded:** 1970, Canoga Park, CA

**Origin:** Started as a 7-seat soup, salad, and sandwich counter in a natural food store. Co-founder Bob created Vegenaise out of a need for egg-free mayo — Earth Island was formed to scale it.

## **3. Target Audiences**

**Primary Persona — "The Conscious Committer":** Values-based plant-based consumer who believes food choices reflect ethics (animal welfare, carbon footprint). Defines healthy food as more-natural and less-processed. Expects plant-based products to taste as good as or better than dairy/egg counterparts. Skews female (61%), high income ($100k+, 24%), ages 18–34 (37%), traditional grocery shopper.

- **Primary:** Vegans and long-term plant-based eaters; ethics/animal welfare motivated; expect products to perform like dairy/egg counterparts; brand-loyal
- **Secondary:** Flexitarians / allergen-avoiders (dairy-free, egg-free, soy-free); KOF-K Kosher Parve (all products)
- **Tertiary:** Family shoppers making plant-based transitions; kids appeal via American-style slices

## **7. Editorial Tone**

**Use:** Warm, mission-driven, quietly passionate, purposeful, approachable and inclusive, confident without preachiness, grounded in a genuine 50-year story.

**Avoid:** Preachy or guilt-tripping about meat/dairy consumption; overly clinical or nutritional-report style; exaggerated health claims; fear-based messaging; exclusionary language (welcome flexitarians and curious consumers, not just committed vegans).

Do not lecture readers about food choices. The brand leads by example and by flavor.

---

## **8. Vocabulary**

**Preferred:**
- Plant-based / Dairy-free / Egg-free / Vegan / Soy-free (when product qualifies)
- Live & active cultures (yogurt)
- Expeller-pressed (oils)
- Made with [specific oil/ingredient]
- Non-GMO Project Verified
- Good source of calcium (cheese: 10% DV per serving; Parmesan 20% DV)
- 100% renewable electricity / powered by renewable energy
- Better Than Mayo® (Vegenaise — registered tagline)

**Never Use:**
- Healthy (as a product claim)
- The best (superlative)
- Natural (use "naturally sourced" or specific ingredient descriptions)
- Guilt-free
- Clean (as in "clean eating" — too vague)
- Dairy alternative (use "dairy-free")

Do not make disease prevention or treatment claims. Calcium and Vitamin D claims are acceptable when nutritionally accurate per serving.

## **9. Compliance & Mandatory Inclusions**

- **Vegenaise®** — use registered ™ on first use per piece
- **Non-GMO Project Verified** — apply only when confirmed for that product
- **Nut-free** — do NOT claim; coconut is present in Sour Cream and some cheese products; cross-contamination risk exists
- **USDA Organic** — apply only to Organic Vegenaise and Organic Caesar Dressing
- **Rainforest Alliance** — apply only to Parmesan products
- **100% renewable electricity** — manufacturing claim only, not a product-level claim
- **Honey Mustard dressing** — use "vegan honey alternative" or "no honey"; it contains no animal honey
- **Soy-free distinction:**
  - Soy-free Vegenaise varieties: Avocado Oil, Chipotle, Soy-Free only
  - All cheese products: soy-free
  - Ranch, Bleu Cheese, Honey Mustard, Caesar dressings: contain soy
- Standard FDA nutritional claim compliance applies throughout

## **11. Content Structure Requirements**

### Product Pages
- SEO-optimized H1: product name + dairy-free/vegan descriptor
- Intro: 2–3 sentences; lead with taste and performance, then certifications (vegan, gluten-free, Non-GMO)
- Feature bullets (3–5): key attribute (Soy-Free / USDA Organic / Expeller-Pressed oil), Non-GMO Project Verified, Dairy-Free & Vegan, Gluten-Free, Good Source of Calcium (cheese: 10% DV; Parmesan 20% DV)
- FAQ: exactly **3 questions** — cover allergens (soy-free? nut-free?), certifications, use-case/cooking functionality
- Vegenaise® trademark on first use

### Recipe Pages
- Lead with the dish, not the product
- Include "Client Information" block for social captions if requested
- Recipe hub categories: Breakfast, Main Dishes, Appetizers, Desserts, Sauces, Vegenaise-specific
- Lifestyle tags: Vegan, Gluten-Free, Soy-Free where applicable

### Blog / Educational Articles
- Lead with the consumer question or pain point
- Ingredient explanations: accessible, not clinical
- Sustainability content: cite real specifics (solar output, Rainforest Alliance, renewable energy)
- Recipe integration encouraged

### Category / Landing Pages
- Lead with product family differentiator (Vegenaise → "the original better mayo"; Cheese → taste + functionality)
- List all active SKUs with clear soy-free/organic callouts
- Include store-locator CTA

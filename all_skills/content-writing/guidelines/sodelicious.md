## **7. Editorial Tone**

**Use:** Playful, spirited, soul-forward ("feed your soul"), mission-driven without lecturing, inclusive, confident, grounded in real sustainability commitments.

**Avoid:** Preachy or guilt-tripping about dairy; health-claim-heavy; vague sustainability language; clinical intros; exaggerated superlatives.

## **11. Content Structure Requirements**

### Product Pages
- SEO-optimized H1: product name + base type (e.g., "Cashewmilk Frozen Dessert — Salted Caramel Cluster")
- Intro: 2–3 sentences; lead with texture/flavor, then certifications
- Feature bullets (3–5): Certified Vegan, Non-GMO Project Verified, Gluten-Free Certified, base type ("Made with cashewmilk"), Rainforest Alliance Certified Cocoa (chocolate only), No Added Sugar (NSA only)
- FAQ: exactly **3 questions** — cover allergens, certifications, NSA sweetener questions, storage

### Recipe Pages
- Lead with dish or occasion
- Highlight dairy-free substitution angle — So Delicious as 1:1 swap
- Tag by lifestyle: Vegan, Gluten-Free, No Sugar Added where applicable
- CocoWhip recipes: dessert toppings, holiday entertaining, smoothie bowls

### Blog / Educational Content
- Lead with consumer question (e.g., "What is coconutmilk ice cream?")
- Sustainability content: cite real data — B Corp, Rainforest Alliance, living wage, renewable electricity
- Do not oversell nutrition — brand edge is values + taste, not health claims

### Category / Landing Pages
- Lead with widest variety claim — most bases, most flavors
- Organize frozen desserts by base type
- Include "Where to Buy" and Flavor Quiz CTAs

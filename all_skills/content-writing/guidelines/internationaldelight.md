# **INTERNATIONAL DELIGHT™ (US MARKET) — AI CONTENT PROFILE**

---

## Brand Overview

- **Brand:** International Delight™
- **Industry:** Flavored coffee creamers and ready-to-drink beverages
- **Market:** United States retail grocery and omnichannel
- **Parent:** Danone North America (Certified B Corporation)
- **Core Products:** Coffee Creamers, Cold Foam Creamers, Zero Sugar Creamers, Ready-to-Drink Iced Coffee
- **Note:** FDA classifies creamers as non-dairy, though they contain sodium caseinate (milk allergen). Labels must state "a milk derivative" after "sodium caseinate."

---

## Editorial Mission

Balance **flavor-forward indulgence** with **Danone's responsible, transparent, science-based framework**. Content must never be overly promotional, exaggerated, morally judgmental, or nutritionally irresponsible.

---

## Target Audience

- Coffee drinkers 18+; at-home café creators; flavor explorers; budget-conscious consumers; Zero Sugar seekers; seasonal shoppers
- Values: customization, flavor innovation, affordable café-style moments, transparency, convenient indulgence

---

## Tone & Style

**Use:** Optimistic, empowering, transparent, confident, informative, flavor-forward, trust-building, purpose-driven

**Avoid:** Overly promotional ("the best ever," "ultimate," "perfect"), clinical, moralizing, guilt-based, excess indulgence language, health-claim heavy

---

## Vocabulary Framework

### Preferred Terms

| Category | Approved vocabulary |
|---|---|
| Nutrition | "nutritious options," "contains…," "source of…," "positive nutrition," "with only X grams of sugar per serving" |
| Product | "coffee enhancer," "cold foam creamer," "Zero Sugar coffee creamer," "ready-to-drink iced coffee," "prepared in the US" |
| Ingredients | "no artificial colors or flavors" (if accurate), "naturally sourced ingredients," "GMO-free" |
| Sensory | creamy, smooth, flavor-forward, café-style, balanced sweetness, bold taste |

### Banned Words (Mandatory)

Never use: healthy, wholesome, wellness, essential, guilt-free, treat, low sugar (standalone), natural (use "naturally sourced"), additives (use "preservatives"), satiety, fresh fruits, made in US (use "prepared in the US")

Never: associate product with "health," make unverified environmental claims, claim medical/physiological benefits, mention International Delight or Danone by name in copy.

---

## Competitor Rules

Competitors: Coffee Mate, Starbucks, Chobani. Never denigrate or compare negatively. Name only in neutral SEO comparison content.

---

## Key Differentiators

1. Extensive flavor variety
2. Seasonal & co-branded launches
3. Cold Foam Creamers expansion
4. Strong Zero Sugar line
5. Café-style experience at home
6. Danone North America B Corp backing

---

## Seasonal Strategy

- **Q1 (Winter):** Hot coffee usage peak
- **Q4 (Holiday):** Pumpkin, Peppermint, festive flavors — highlight limited-time availability, seasonal rituals; drive urgency without pressure tactics

## Content Detail by Type

| Type | Required elements |
|---|---|
| Product pages | Flavor description, texture, suggested use, precise nutrition highlights, transparent ingredient positioning, sustainability paragraph |

## Pitfalls to Avoid

- Overusing indulgence language or sounding like dessert marketing
- Incorrect sugar-related claims
- UK spelling (use US: flavor, color)
- Exaggerated environmental claims
- Children's brand voice

### FAQ Rules by Page Type

| Page type | Has FAQ? | Heading format |
|---|---|---|
| Individual product pages (flavor SKUs) | Yes | "Frequently Asked Questions About Our [Product]" or "[Flavor] Frequently Asked Questions" |
| Product-line / category pages | Yes | Conversational: "All Your [Product] Questions Answered!" |
| Broad collection pages (e.g., top-level Coffee Creamers) | No | — |
| Recipe pages | Yes | "Questions Related to the [Recipe]" |

- Always 3 questions per FAQ (maintain existing count if page already has more)
- Product pages: taste profile, calorie count, sugar content, ingredients/allergens, use in baking/cooking
- Recipe pages: variations, substitutions, storage, serving suggestions

### Product Page Content Structure (Individual SKUs)
1. **H1** — product name
2. **Intro paragraph** — 1–2 sentences; playful, warm, flavor-forward
3. **FAQ section** — 3 questions
4. **Nutritional Information** — serving size + full nutrition panel
5. **Ingredients list**

### Product-Line / Category Page Content Structure
1. **Punchy headline** — playful, fun, second-person questions or exclamations
2. **Short intro** — 2–3 sentences; inviting, warm, slightly whimsical; highlight flavor variety and formats
3. **Product range description** — mention flavors and formats; conversational
4. **Closing invitation** — direct CTA phrasing ("Pour, stir, and enjoy your cup your way")
5. **FAQ section** — 3 questions

### Recipe Page Content Structure
1. **Intro** — brief, flavor-forward; mentions the ID product used
2. **Tips section** — "Tips Before Making Your [Recipe]" with H3 subheadings
3. **Recipe block** — Prep time, total time, servings, Ingredients list, Directions (numbered steps)
4. **FAQ section** — "Questions Related to the [Recipe]" — 3 questions (variations, substitutions, storage, serving suggestions)

- Avoid: clinical language, overly serious nutrition claims, children's brand voice

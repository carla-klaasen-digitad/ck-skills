## **2. Tone of Voice (TOV) Guidelines**

### TOV 1: SPEAK OUR MIND *(Defy Every Expectation)*
Own our space with out-loud declarations that leave no room for second-guessing. Assert truths plainly in a confident frame — never preach or lecture. Keep imperative voice in the mix. ("Stronger makes everything better." / "Perform. Eat. Repeat.")

### TOV 2: PUMP UP THE POSITIVE *(Amplify Real Strength)*
100% positive energy and respect for anyone who wants to get strong. Get a little clever. Empower every type of strength — resist stereotypes. ("Unstoppability, now in strawberry." ✅ vs. "Beach body fuel, now in strawberry." ✗)

### TOV 3: CUT TO THE CHASE *(Unleash a Playful Edge)*
Hit the mark with few well-chosen words. Go big on fragments and choppy construction — conventional narrative sentences are a conscious choice, not the default. ("The protein helps. All 15 grams of it." ✅ vs. "We pile on the power to fuel your day with 15 grams of protein." ✗) When in doubt, cut it out.

### WE ARE / WE ARE NOT

| WE ARE | WE ARE NOT |
|---|---|
| **All about swagger** — bold conviction and style | **Braggy** — we write to show UP, not show off |
| **Ambitious** — celebrates big goals, everyone's | **Domineering** — never preach, push, or claim to know better |
| **Unconventional** — cues from spoken word, not hung up on "rules" | **Rebellious** — not out to shock or antagonize |
| **Irreverent** — it's okay to have fun | **Too cool for school** — wit is never dismissive, judgy, or unkind |

**Punctuation rule:** No period in headlines is the default. Exceptions use periods for emphasis. Always follow Copy Library exactly — even removing/editing a single word requires brand review.

**Slang:** Approved brand-adjacent terms: maverick, charisma, swagger, ambitious, unconventional, irreverent. When unsure if slang aligns, skip it.

## **3. Influencer Do's & Don'ts** *(V3.31.2026)*

✅ **DO:**
- Ensure all content is **evergreen** (no references to Big Game, specific dates, or time of year)
- Say **"per serving"** when referencing protein
- Highlight **high protein**
- Keep educational points **simple and easy to understand**
- If implying protein helps build muscle/strength, include the required disclaimer
- Use **"g"** or **"grams"** for nutrition amounts (e.g., 20g protein, 0g added sugar)

🚫 **DON'T:**
- For off-site guest posts: **never include the brand, Danone brand names, or product name** — only the anchor provided
- Do not mention or speak negatively about other brands
- Do not reference sponsored events, celebrities, or pop culture moments
- Do not compare OIKOS to other yogurt brands
- Do not claim OIKOS directly improves athletic performance, increases strength, or boosts energy on its own
- Do not give one-size-fits-all nutrition advice or daily protein recommendations
- Never say **"0g sugar"** or **"zero sugar"** — only **"0g added sugar"**
- Do not refer to OIKOS as a **"yogurt brand"**
- Do not call **OIKOS PRO** "yogurt" — use **"snack"** or **"dairy snack"** *(OIKOS Triple Zero may be called "yogurt")*
- Do not use: **perfect, refuel, recovery** *(for energy/performance fueling claims; "muscle recovery" and "supporting muscles" are still acceptable for PRO products with approved language)*

## **11. OIKOS PRO® Naming Conventions — Legal Compliance**

FDA Standards of Identity (21 CFR 131.200) require that any product calling itself "yogurt" must be made from standard fluid milk. OIKOS PRO® uses ultra-filtered milk as its base and adds whey protein isolate — disqualifying it from the "yogurt" or "Greek yogurt" identities.

### ✅ Permitted Terms

| Term | Usage |
|---|---|
| **"Yogurt-Cultured Ultra-Filtered Milk Product"** | Primary identity label — exact Danone packaging language; always acceptable |
| **"Yogurt-Cultured Dairy Snack"** | Secondary descriptor only — never use as primary front-of-pack identity |
| **"Cultured Dairy Product"** | Safe as a process/category descriptor |
| **"High-Protein Dairy Product"** | Safe — describes characteristics without claiming SOI identity |
| **"Ultra-Filtered Milk Product"** | Accurate and legally defensible |
| **"Dairy Snack"** / **"Snack"** | Already approved in brand guidelines (see Section 3) |
| **"Protein Snack"** / **"High-Protein Snack"** | Safe as marketing descriptors |

### 🚫 Prohibited Terms

| Term | Why Prohibited |
|---|---|
| **"Yogurt"** (standalone) | Uses ultra-filtered milk base + added whey protein isolate — fails FDA SOI |
| **"Greek Yogurt"** | Not made via traditional straining process — fails FDA SOI |
| **"Traditional Greek Yogurt"** | Same as above |
| **"Strained Yogurt"** | Same as above |
| **"Milk"** (standalone) | Product has been heavily altered and fermented — misleading |

> **Note:** "Yogurt-cultured" as a hyphenated adjective is permitted because it describes the manufacturing process, not the product identity. It must not appear as the sole primary identifier.
